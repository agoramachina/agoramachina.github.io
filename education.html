Academic Reflection

The most exciting phrase to hear in science, the one that heralds new discoveries, is not “Eureka” but “That’s funny...”

--Isaac Asimov


Curiosity is central to who I am. Whether personally, professionally, or academically, I find myself compelled by an obsessive drive to seek knowledge wherever it can be found. This drive has led to a passion for learning both in and out of school, combining the structure of formal education with the freedom of independent learning. My approach is eclectic: although the resources and tools I have obtained throughout my academic career at UCF have been invaluable in grounding and guiding my research, my primary source of knowledge is through the pursuit of my own interests.

I enjoy taking a learning-through-doing approach in my pursuit of knowledge, HUDbeat being a primary example. I initially set out to create a tool that could assist in understanding and shaping my own mind, which required a thorough review of scholarly articles on psychology, neuroscience, and technology. I programmed an Arduino to detect heart rate and used years of self-taught Linux expertise to control the Raspberry Pi that served as the “brains” of the project. Designing the hardware required knowledge of Fritzing, OpenSCAD, and Inventor, which I learned in order to model and build the electronic systems and their casings. As a work-in-progress, there is a great deal I look forward to learning as I continue the project. Some of these knowledge gaps are known, with a plan to fill them. But most exciting to me are the unknown unknowns: knowledge that I’m not yet aware of lacking, but look forward to discovering!

Course of Study

Humanities

PHH 3460 - Modern Western Philosophy

A comparative analysis of rationalist and empiricist philosophies from 17th century to modern day.

PHI 3626 - Adv. Ethics in Science and Technology

An ethical and sociological examination of science and technology with a focus on theoretical and applied philosophy in contemporary issues.

PHI 3638 - Ethical Issues in the 21st Century

An exploration of ethical approaches to contemporary social issues by examining the moral and social impacts of current technologies.

PHI 3670 - Ethical Theory

Theories and applications of modern and historical ethical models. The primary author of study was Shafer-Landau, who analyzes these moral theories and argues for moral realism.

PHI 4400 - Philosophy of Science

Understanding the philosophical basis of scientific thought by identifying the methodologies and conceptual underpinnings of scientific reasoning. Particular attention was given to the role of Kuhn's "paradigm shifts" in established and revolutionary science. This class also fulfills the "Being and Knowing" requirement for a Philosophy minor.

Computer Science

COP 3223 - Intro to Programming with C

Foundations, concepts, and applied implementation in the C programming language. Explored general paradigms of Object Oriented programming and linked list data structures.

STA 2023 - Statistical Methods I

An introduction to probability and concepts in statistical theory. Learned how to apply statistical methods in the interpretion and analysis of data.

COT 3100C - Intro to Discrete Structures

Foundations of formal logic as applied to discrete mathematics. Introduced the syntax and structure of formal logical proofs and demonstrated methods of determining truth values of logical and mathematical statements. Learned how to typeset with LaTeX for better mathematical notetaking.

MAC 2311 - Calculus w/ Analytic Geometry I

An introduction to single variable calculus through the concepts of limits, derivatives and integrals. Incorporated both theory and application. Learned how to use Mathematica to model and analyze mathematical functions.

MAC 23112 - Calculus w/ Analytic Geometry II

Advanced applications of derivatives and integrals. Defined infinite sequences and series as well as methods for determining their convergence and divergence. Also introduced non-Cartesian coordinate systems. Learned to use Mathematica to graph and analyze multi-dimensional functions.

Philosophy

PHI 2108 - Critical Thinking

Application of critical thinking in thought and action through the study of informal fallacies as used in conversation and reasoning. This class fulfills the "Reasoning" requirement for a Philosophy minor.

PHH 3100 - Ancient Philosophy

Studied the primary texts of Classical Greek philosophers including Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle. Explored schools of thought such as Platonism, Stoicism, and Cynicism, as well as early philosophical foundations of science. This class fulfills the "History of Philosophy" requirement for a Philosophy minor.

Cognitive Science

ZOO 3744 - Neurobiology

Studied the macro and micro physiology of the brain and central nervous system. Covered vision and auditory perception as well as cell and nerve structure and function. I designed and crocheted a model of the visual system, including the eye and its musculature and nerve structure. This class fulfills the "Neuroscience" requirement for the Cognitive Science minor.

PHI 3323 - Minds and Machines

An interdisciplinary approach to the study of cognitive science. Unified perspectives from fields such as philosophy, psychology, neuroscience, and artificial intelligence. Covered various models of mind developed in the 20th and 21st century, such as CRUM, connectionism, and 4E cognition. This class counts as an elective for the Philosophy minor and fulfills the "Philosophy" requirement for a Cognitive Science minor.

EXP 3604 - Cognitive Psychology

Studied topics and methodologies in cognitive psychology. This class explored cognitive phenomena such as perception, attention, memory, and knowledge. Studies and experiments were also used to demonstrate emperical approaches to cognition. This class fulfills the "Psychology" requirement for a Cognitive Science minor.
